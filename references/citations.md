# The evidence pack

SESHAT's methods are not vibes; each core mechanism has published evidence behind it. Cite one line when the method is used, never more.

## Pre-mortem (lens 8)

- **Klein, G. (2007). "Performing a Project Premortem." Harvard Business Review, 85(9), 18-19.**
  The protocol: the team assumes the plan has ALREADY failed spectacularly, then independently writes every plausible reason why. Legitimizes the dissent that planning momentum suppresses. https://hbr.org/2007/09/performing-a-project-premortem
- **Mitchell, D.J., Russo, J.E., & Pennington, N. (1989). "Back to the future: Temporal perspective in the explanation of events." Journal of Behavioral Decision Making, 2(1), 25-38.**
  Prospective hindsight (imagining the outcome as certain and past) increases correct identification of reasons for future outcomes by roughly 30%. The empirical basis Klein cites.

Inline cite: `pre-mortem: Klein HBR 2007; ~30% better failure-reason identification, Mitchell 1989`

## Adversarial council (War Council mode)

- **Chiang, C.-W., Lu, Z., Li, Z., & Yin, M. (2024). "Enhancing AI-Assisted Group Decision Making through LLM-Powered Devil's Advocate." Proc. ACM IUI '24, 103-119. DOI 10.1145/3640543.3645199.**
  Verified finding: groups with an LLM devil's advocate challenging the AI's recommendation achieved significantly higher decision accuracy and more appropriate reliance on (biased) AI advice. Honest caveat: accuracy gains concentrated on in-distribution tasks; devil's advocacy reduces overreliance but can increase under-reliance.
- **Schweiger, D.M., Sandberg, W.R., & Ragan, J.W. (1986). "Group Approaches for Improving Strategic Decision Making." Academy of Management Journal, 29(1), 51-71.**
  Devil's advocacy and dialectical inquiry both produced higher-quality recommendations and assumptions than consensus review (at a cost to participant satisfaction). The canonical support for structured adversarial process.

Inline cite: `adversarial review beats consensus: Schweiger 1986; LLM devil's advocate improves accuracy: Chiang et al., IUI'24`
